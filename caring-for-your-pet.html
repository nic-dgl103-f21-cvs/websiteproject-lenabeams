Importance of Dog Nail Trims

In a perfect world, dogs should get their nails trimmed about once a month, but life can get busy and things like this can easily get forgotten. However, if it has been a long time since your pups last nail trim, and their nails aren’t getting worn down on pavement, they do become a risk of getting pulled off, or becoming painfully outgrown and having the quicks ‘grow -out’ with the nail. This results, in uncomfortable long nails, that touch the ground with force every time they take a step, (ouch!) which long-term, could alter the way their foot is placed on the ground, and making the paw look ‘splayed’ out. Unfortunately, once the quick is grown out, you can’t trim off as much as you’d like, or you risk cutting their quicks and they will bleed. If it gets to this point, which is very common, you have to ‘train’ the quick to recede back with regular nail trimmings- and this could take a while, but it is worth it for the comfort of your dog. A good rule of thumb to follow is, if you can hear your dog’s toenails hit the ground as they walk, they need a trim.

About Cat Nail Trims

Cats don’t always need Nail Trims, if they are hunters, or outside cats that don’t often roam around indoors. However, when your feline friend starts scratching up your furniture, carpet- or even you, that would be a good indication to think about getting them trimmed. Some cats aren’t the biggest fan while taking away their talons, but once it’s done, they really don’t care at all. It’s best to get them acclimated to having their paws touched and toes trimmed as a kitten to avoid the fear of it, but if this is not the case, I have a few tricks up my sleeve, and am up for the task.
